# iChart Appearance Directions

Date: June 6, 2026

Purpose: explore a few global app appearance directions using the locked B4.8A-H1 logo and color usage rules.

## A. Stage Workbench

Closest to the current Library home-screen trial. Uses a strong dark brand header/hero, warm paper rows, Blue Soft selection, and iChart Blue for primary actions.

Best fit:
- first rollout
- Library/home screen
- product surfaces that need a clear brand moment

Risk:
- if over-applied, the app could become too dark or too hero-heavy.

## B. Paper Studio

Most chart-first and least branded. Uses warm paper as the dominant surface, a light-surface wordmark, restrained iChart Blue actions, and minimal dark framing.

Best fit:
- long writing sessions
- education/studio contexts
- chart editing surfaces where comfort matters more than brand drama

Risk:
- may feel less distinctive unless typography, spacing, and the logo mark stay sharp.

## C. Rehearsal Console

Most operational/professional. Uses a dark navigation rail and command framing around a warm paper chart page.

Best fit:
- editor mode
- repeated action workflows
- live/rehearsal confidence

Risk:
- the rail must stay framing-only; too much dark chrome could fight the paper-first rule.

## Current Read

The strongest product direction is now locked for the home page as a hybrid:

- A/B become light and dark modes.
- C contributes the app-level sidebar navigation.
- The header stays closer to A/B.
- Final tab labels and ordering are a later tuning pass.

That hybrid follows the usage rule: dark frames the instrument, paper holds the music.
