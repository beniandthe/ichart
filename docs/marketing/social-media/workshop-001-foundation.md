# iChart Social Media Workshop 001: Foundation

Status: first audience locked
Date: 2026-07-15
Pipeline: `docs/marketing/social-media/social-media-workshop-pipeline.md`

## Purpose

Start at the beginning: decide what the social and Apple marketing system is supposed to prove before we make individual posts.

The goal is not to "do social media" in the abstract. The goal is to find the first musicians who immediately understand why iChart matters, then use their reactions to sharpen the launch story.

## Starting Thesis

iChart should be introduced as a practical iPad chart-writing tool for working musicians:

> Handwrite clean charts at paper speed, then transpose, copy, export, store, and edit them like structured iPad charts.

This is the center of gravity. Every early post should prove one part of it visually.

## Active Channels

Primary creation lanes:

- TikTok: fastest discovery and raw workflow testing.
- Instagram Reels: polished visual proof and musician discovery.
- Instagram Stories: build/test status, polls, beta asks, quick trust.
- Instagram Carousels: explainers, comparisons, workflow education.
- Apple Marketing: App Store screenshots, app previews, custom product pages, badge-safe launch assets.

Supporting lanes:

- LinkedIn: build-in-public credibility and educator/music-business context.
- Facebook: music groups, teachers, local/gigging communities, and longer feedback prompts.
- Threads: lightweight conversation and fast product notes.
- Website/landing content: trusted handoff from social to beta/App Store action.

Parked:

- YouTube Shorts is out of scope for now.

## First Audience Hypotheses

### 1. Working Bandleaders And Gigging Musicians

Pain:
- Need clean charts quickly.
- Last-minute rehearsal or gig prep.
- Paper is fast, but paper charts are hard to reuse, hard to transpose, hard to store, and hard to send cleanly.
- Need to hand something to other players without overbuilding a full notation file.

Proof to show:
- Handwrite one chart quickly and cleanly.
- Transpose or copy it for different instruments or keys.
- Export/send PDFs without rewriting the chart.
- Store it for future edits.

Best channels:
- TikTok, Instagram Reels, Facebook groups, Apple custom product page.

Status:
- Locked as the first audience wedge.

### 2. Teachers

Pain:
- Need simplified charts for students.
- Need clean PDFs without full notation overhead.
- Need to correct and transpose quickly.

Proof to show:
- Simple chart built from scratch.
- Clear PDF export.
- Student-friendly readable result.

Best channels:
- Instagram carousel, Facebook, LinkedIn, Apple custom product page.

### 3. Rhythm Section And Session Players

Pain:
- Need roadmaps, chords, and form to be readable for rhythm-section/session work.
- Need clear chart placement without full notation engraving.
- Need transposition support.

Proof to show:
- Beat-aware chord placement.
- Roadmap markings.
- Concert/Bb/Eb view or transpose workflow.

Best channels:
- TikTok, Instagram Reels, LinkedIn, Apple custom product page.

## What We Need To Learn First

1. Which audience recognizes the problem fastest?
2. Which visual proof makes the app click without explanation?
3. Which phrase feels most natural to musicians?
4. Which channel brings real musician feedback instead of empty views?
5. Which social proof angle should become App Store screenshot or app-preview copy?

## Messaging Guardrails

Say:
- iPad-first chart writing.
- Apple Pencil workflow.
- Chord charts for rhythm-section and gig workflows.
- Clean readable PDF export.
- Practical rehearsal and gig prep.
- Not full notation software.

Do not imply:
- Full notation engraving.
- Melody or pitched note entry.
- Playback/backing tracks.
- Collaboration at launch.
- AI magic or perfect recognition.
- Cross-platform support at launch.

## First Creative Bet

The first creative bet should be a gig-workflow proof:

1. Show the real bandleader/gigging-musician pressure: charts are needed quickly for multiple players or keys.
2. Show Apple Pencil chart writing in iChart at handwriting speed.
3. Show structured chart payoff: transpose, copy, export PDF, and keep it for later.
4. Show the charts ready to send to the band.
5. End with a quiet CTA: "Testing on iPad now" or "Built for working musicians."

Why this bet:
- It proves the core promise quickly.
- It works on TikTok and Instagram Reels.
- It can become an Instagram carousel.
- It can inform App Store screenshots and custom product page copy.

## First Workshop Decisions

Decision 1: First audience to bias toward.

Decision:
- Start with working bandleaders and gigging musicians because the need is urgent, visual, and emotionally obvious.

Alternates:
- Teachers remain a strong second lane for trust and educational use.
- Rhythm section/session players remain a strong feature-proof lane once we have stronger capture evidence for chart placement and roadmap workflows.

Decision 2: First proof asset to capture.

Recommendation:
- One write-once workflow: create a clean iChart chart quickly, transpose/copy it for another part or key, then export/share PDFs.

Decision 3: First CTA.

Recommendation:
- Before public App Store release: "Join the iPad beta" or "Testing on iPad now."
- After public App Store release: Apple-approved "Download on the App Store" path.

Decision 4: First measurement target.

Recommendation:
- Qualitative signal first: comments and DMs from real musicians who describe a use case.
- Secondary signal: saves/shares, then link clicks.

## Starter Content Sequence

Post 1: Write Once, Transpose, Send
- Format: TikTok + Instagram Reel.
- Audience: bandleaders/gigging musicians.
- Hook: "Write one chart. Transpose it for the band. Send the PDFs."
- CTA: "Testing on iPad now."

Post 2: Why Chart Placement Matters
- Format: Instagram carousel + LinkedIn.
- Audience: rhythm section/session players and teachers.
- Hook: "Chord charts are not just chord names. Placement matters."
- CTA: "Follow the beta build."

Post 3: Basic Is Real Local Writing
- Format: Instagram carousel + Facebook/Threads.
- Audience: cautious musicians evaluating trust.
- Hook: "Basic should let musicians feel the tool before paying."
- CTA: "Read launch notes" or "Join beta updates."
