# iChart Social Media And Apple Marketing Workshop Pipeline

Status: v1 operating system for social media and Apple marketing, excluding YouTube Shorts for now
Research pass: 2026-07-15
Product authority: `README.md`, `docs/branding/ichart/brand-system.md`, `docs/app-store-testflight-metadata-draft.md`, `docs/monetization-strategy.md`

## Purpose

This document turns the social media effort into a repeatable pipeline: workshop ideas, produce assets, publish by channel, measure results, and feed the learning back into the next batch.

The system is designed for iChart: an iPad-first, Apple Pencil chart-writing app for working musicians. The social output should feel like a musician showing a useful tool at rehearsal, not a generic SaaS launch.

## Thread Operating Mode

Use this thread as the working room for four kinds of requests:

- Workshop: turn a rough topic, launch milestone, tester comment, or product behavior into post angles.
- Produce: turn an approved angle into scripts, shot lists, captions, carousels, video briefs, and asset QA.
- Implement: build calendar entries, landing-page copy, App Store custom product page ideas, scheduler checklists, and campaign measurement sheets.
- Review: inspect drafted posts, screenshots, videos, captions, or performance data against this pipeline and current platform guidance.

Default flow: idea -> production brief -> capture/edit checklist -> platform copy -> publish checklist -> measurement retro.

Active lanes:
- TikTok: discovery, native short-form demos, fast audience learning.
- Instagram: visual proof, Reels, carousels, Stories, beta/community trust.
- Apple marketing: App Store product page, custom product pages, badges, screenshots, app previews, Apple Ads readiness.
- LinkedIn: credibility with educators, music business peers, Apple ecosystem observers, and build-in-public product followers.
- Facebook: musician groups, teachers, local/gigging community, and longer feedback prompts.
- Threads: lightweight conversation, quick build notes, and cross-posted discussion starters when useful.
- Website/landing content: conversion support, campaign continuity, and trusted destinations for social traffic.

Parked lanes:
- YouTube Shorts is parked for now. It can be revisited later if audience evidence changes, but it should not drive capture, edit, cadence, or measurement decisions in this phase.

## Research Synthesis

1. Vertical video is the default creative surface.
   - Use 9:16 as the master format for Instagram Reels and TikTok.
   - Export a clean 1080x1920 master and adapt downward, rather than building separate one-off assets first.
   - Keep captions, CTAs, the iChart wordmark, and important UI details away from platform chrome and lower captions.

2. The hook has to arrive immediately.
   - TikTok recommends introducing the content proposition in the first 3 seconds and prioritizing the hook in the first 6 seconds.
   - Instagram Reels creative guidance points in the same practical direction: vertical, audio, safe-zone clarity, and fast visual comprehension.
   - iChart hooks should be concrete: "Write one chart, transpose it for the horn section, send PDFs" beats "A new way to write music charts."

3. Platform-native beats over-polished.
   - TikTok recommends a DIY, not overly polished style, with sound, people, and platform-native text overlays.
   - For iChart, that means hands, iPad screen recordings, rehearsal-room context, mistakes being corrected, and real export results.
   - The brand system still applies: dark stage, warm paper, precise notation, blue accent. The tone can be casual; the visuals should stay disciplined.

4. Consistency needs a calendar, tags, and a queue.
   - Buffer's management guidance starts with strategy, audience, pillars, platform choice, cadence, tagging, ideas, scheduling, and performance review.
   - LinkedIn's small-business guidance recommends a monthly calendar, analytics review, and a 3-2-1 model: three industry posts, two community/pride posts, one product/service post per week.
   - For the active lanes, track TikTok, Instagram Reels, Instagram Stories, Instagram carousels, LinkedIn, Facebook/Threads, website/landing assets, and Apple product-page assets separately.
   - iChart should run a sustainable cadence first, then increase volume only when the capture/edit/review loop is stable.

5. App marketing needs channel-specific landing paths.
   - Apple supports App Store custom product pages, including unique URLs for different audiences and campaigns.
   - Product page optimization can test up to three alternate product page versions against the original.
   - Social campaigns should not all point to the same generic destination once App Store pages are live. Use channel and audience specific links where possible.

6. Music and chart rights need stricter handling than normal app marketing.
   - Use original demo charts, public-domain material, or fully cleared user examples.
   - Do not show full copyrighted charts, private student material, setlists, emails, account details, or paid forum content without permission.
   - Use platform-cleared audio libraries or original/royalty-free audio, and preserve proof of audio/chart rights for any paid or App Store-facing creative.

## Positioning

### Core Promise

Write naturally with Apple Pencil. iChart turns practical chord chart ideas into clean, editable, exportable iPad charts.

### Short Message Bank

- Pencil-first charts for iPad.
- Handwrite clean charts at paper speed.
- Write once, transpose, copy, export, and edit later.
- Chord charts with clear measure placement, without becoming full notation software.
- Fast enough for gig prep. Structured enough for the bandstand.

### Audience Segments

- Working bandleaders who need charts quickly.
- Rhythm section players who need readable roadmaps and quick PDFs.
- Teachers creating simplified charts for students.
- Session players and gigging musicians who need quick, reliable PDFs.
- Music educators and creators who can explain the workflow to others.

### Tone

Musician-practical, specific, calm, and quietly confident. Avoid hype claims like "revolutionary notation AI" or anything that implies full notation, playback, melody entry, or collaboration at launch.

## Channel Strategy

### Instagram

Role: visual proof, musician discovery, lightweight community.

Primary formats:
- Reels: workflow demos, before/after transformations, export reveals.
- Stories: daily build/test snippets, polls, beta feedback prompts, behind-the-scenes.
- Carousels: mini tutorials, "paper chart vs iChart" comparisons, feature explainers.

Default CTA:
- "Join the TestFlight list" before public launch.
- "Download on the App Store" after release, using Apple's badge rules.

Operating notes:
- Reels and TikTok can share the same captured action, but captions, hooks, cover frames, and audio choices should be reviewed separately.
- Carousels should carry tutorial or comparison ideas that need more than one screen.
- Stories are the best place for build status, polls, tester prompts, and quick reposts of strong Reels.

### TikTok

Role: discovery, fast education, native demos.

Primary formats:
- 9-30 second raw workflow demos.
- Mistake/correction loops.
- "POV: the bandleader needs charts for the whole band before rehearsal" scenarios.
- Creator/teacher stitches only where rights and brand fit are clear.

Creative rules:
- Hook in first 1-3 seconds.
- Show the product on screen.
- Use voiceover and captions.
- Keep it native and human, not an ad pasted into TikTok.

### Apple Marketing

Role: conversion, trust, review readiness, and channel-specific landing.

Primary surfaces:
- App Store product page metadata, screenshots, and app previews.
- Custom product pages for specific audiences and campaigns.
- Product page optimization tests once there is enough traffic.
- Apple-approved App Store badges, QR codes, and product images.
- Apple Ads creative and audience-specific landing paths after organic learning identifies strong angles.

Default audience pages:
- Bandleaders: fast rehearsal prep, transposed parts, and PDF sharing.
- Teachers: simplified charts for students and readable export.
- Rhythm section/session players: roadmaps, form cues, transposition, and quick correction.

Apple rules:
- Use Apple-provided badge artwork without modification, animation, or visual dominance.
- Keep App Store screenshots and app previews truthful to the shipping app.
- Keep TestFlight-only claims separate from public App Store claims.
- Use custom product page URLs or campaign tags so TikTok and Instagram traffic can be evaluated separately.

### LinkedIn

Role: credibility with educators, music business peers, Apple ecosystem/product observers, and thoughtful build-in-public readers.

Primary formats:
- Build-in-public notes grounded in real musician QA.
- Music education workflows.
- Product lessons from release testing and design tradeoffs.
- PDF/carousel explainers adapted from Instagram carousels.

Cadence:
- 1-2 posts per week, not daily noise.
- Use iChart as a practical product story, not a generic startup victory lap.

### Facebook

Role: musician groups, teachers, local/gigging community, and longer-form feedback loops.

Primary formats:
- Page reposts from Instagram when the content translates cleanly.
- Group participation where allowed by group rules.
- Longer posts asking for specific workflow feedback.
- Teacher/bandleader-oriented posts with a concrete demo or question.

Guardrail:
- Do not spam groups. Lead with a useful demo or question, and follow each group's promotion rules.

### Threads

Role: lightweight conversation, build notes, product observations, and fast follow-up from Instagram/LinkedIn ideas.

Primary formats:
- Short build notes.
- "What would you use this for?" questions.
- Screenshot plus one practical observation.
- Replies to musician/product conversations where iChart has a relevant point of view.

Guardrail:
- Threads should support conversation, not become a second caption dump for every Reel.

### Website And Landing Content

Role: conversion support and continuity between social posts, App Store pages, and beta/App Store actions.

Requirements:
- `useichart.com` should explain the product promise in the same words social uses.
- Build separate landing paths for major audiences: bandleaders, teachers, and rhythm section/session players.
- Use campaign tags or App Store custom product page URLs when available.
- Match Apple-facing copy, pricing claims, and Basic/Pro policy exactly.

### Parked Channels

YouTube Shorts is intentionally out of scope for now. Do not create Shorts-specific titles, descriptions, thumbnails, or upload tasks unless this decision is revisited.

## Content Pillars

1. Handwritten To Structured Chart
   - Show handwriting becoming editable chord/chart objects.
   - Best for Reels, TikTok, app previews, and App Store screenshots.

2. Real Rehearsal Utility
   - Solve a musician problem: last-minute chart writing, transposition, PDF distribution, or chart-version cleanup.
   - Best for TikTok, Instagram Reels, LinkedIn/Facebook posts, and audience-specific custom product pages.

3. Write Once, Reuse
   - Handwritten chart entry, then transposed/exported iChart versions.
   - Best for Reels, TikTok, carousels, screenshots, and app preview storyboards.

4. Chord Placement Matters
   - Show why chord placement inside the chart matters without promising full notation.
   - Best for educators, gigging players, and product explainers.

5. Pro Trust Layer
   - Cloud backup, restore, Projects, Forums, and why Basic still includes real local writing/export.
   - Best for Instagram carousels, launch FAQs, LinkedIn/Facebook context posts, App Store promotional text, and Apple custom product pages.

6. Community And Forum
   - Reviewed PDF snapshots, public attribution, voting/comments/reports when ready.
   - Best after the forum flow is stable and externally understandable.

7. Build In Public
   - Show real testing, musician feedback, TestFlight learning, and craft decisions.
   - Best for LinkedIn, Threads, Instagram Stories, and occasional TikTok/Reels when the story has a clear product visual.

## Creative System

### Master Formats

- Vertical master video: 1080x1920, 9:16, mp4/mov.
- Square backup: 1080x1080 for profile grids or non-primary placements.
- Feed crop: 1080x1350 or equivalent 4:5 for image/carousel when needed.
- Still image: export from the vertical master plus a clean app/PDF screenshot.

### Visual Rules

- Show actual app surfaces, chart pages, Pencil input, and exported PDFs.
- Keep text overlays short, high-contrast, and centered enough to survive UI overlays.
- Use iChart brand colors as accents, not full-screen color washes.
- Keep the B4.8A-H1 wordmark clean and subordinate to the content.
- Do not animate or alter the App Store badge. Use one badge per layout or video when promoting availability.

### Story Structures

#### Problem, Gesture, Result

1. Problem: "I need charts for the band fast."
2. Gesture: handwrite the chart directly in iChart.
3. Result: transpose/copy/export/store the chart.
4. CTA: "Testing on iPad now" or "Download on the App Store."

#### Mistake, Correction, Relief

1. User writes at natural handwriting speed.
2. iChart creates structured chart objects that can be edited.
3. User corrects, transposes, copies, or exports without rewriting the page.
4. CTA: "Built for gig-speed chart writing."

#### Feature Contrast

1. Paper or rigid typed-entry friction.
2. iChart workflow.
3. Who it helps.
4. CTA.

#### Musician POV

1. "POV: the singer moved three songs to new keys."
2. Write quick iReal Pro-style charts.
3. Transpose on the fly and export/share PDFs.
4. CTA.

## End-To-End Workflow

### 1. Monthly Workshop

Inputs:
- Launch/release state.
- Product gates and known limitations.
- Beta/App Store availability.
- Audience priorities.
- What proof assets are available: simulator clips, device captures, screenshots, PDFs, user quotes.

Output:
- One campaign theme.
- 4 weekly subthemes.
- 12-20 candidate post ideas.
- Required capture list.

### 2. Weekly Workshop

Inputs:
- Last week's results.
- Product changes or release blockers.
- Current questions from testers/users.

Output:
- 3-5 approved posts.
- 1-2 reactive slots left open.
- Per-post brief using `production-brief-template.md`.

### 3. Script And Shot Planning

Every short-form post needs:
- Hook line.
- On-screen action.
- Voiceover or caption text.
- CTA.
- Landing path.
- Rights check.
- Platform variants.

### 4. Capture

Capture sources:
- iPad screen recording.
- Physical iPad/Pencil hand shot.
- Exported PDF preview.
- App Store/TestFlight screenshot when allowed.
- Founder/musician talking head only when it adds clarity.

Rules:
- Use demo charts unless rights are cleared.
- Hide private account, email, support, and forum identity details.
- Capture enough extra padding around the key action for safe-zone crops.

### 5. Edit

Default edit recipe:
- 0-2s: hook text plus immediate visual motion.
- 2-12s: core product action.
- 12-20s: result and benefit.
- Final 2-4s: CTA.

Editing checklist:
- Captions or text overlays are readable on mobile.
- Audio is clear and licensed.
- UI detail is zoomed enough to understand.
- CTA is not hidden under platform UI.
- Export a vertical master and any required crops.

### 6. Review

Review gates:
- Product truth: no unsupported launch claims.
- Rights: no copyrighted/private chart material.
- Brand: no off-system logo/color usage.
- Platform: safe zones, length, aspect ratio, caption fit.
- Apple: App Store badge/product-image rules followed when used.
- Accessibility: captions, contrast, alt text/caption context.

### 7. Publish Or Schedule

Recommended tooling sequence:
- Meta Business Suite or Instagram-native scheduling for Instagram planning where available.
- Buffer or another scheduler for cross-channel calendar, tags, queue, and analytics once accounts are connected.
- Manual TikTok publish when native sound/trend selection matters.
- App Store Connect for App Store metadata, screenshots, app previews, custom product pages, product page optimization, and campaign links.
- LinkedIn and Facebook native publishing or scheduler support for longer posts and group-safe variants.
- Threads native posting when conversation timing matters.

Publishing rules:
- Tailor copy per platform.
- Do not cross-post identical captions blindly.
- Preserve a reactive slot each week.
- Reply to meaningful comments within 24 hours when possible.

### 8. Measure

Measurement windows:
- 24 hours: hook/readability and early comments.
- 7 days: reach, retention, saves, shares, profile taps, link clicks.
- 30 days: follower quality, site/App Store actions, TestFlight/app downloads, paid conversion if applicable.

Metrics by funnel:
- Awareness: reach, impressions, 3-second views, hold rate, profile visits.
- Engagement: saves, shares, comments, reposts, follows per post.
- Intent: link clicks, beta requests, App Store product page views, custom product page taps.
- Conversion: installs, trials, Pro starts, support/contact signals.
- Quality: comments from actual musicians, teachers, or bandleaders; feedback usefulness; support load.

Platform-specific metrics:
- TikTok: retention curve, average watch time, full watch rate, shares, profile views, follows, comments from musicians, link taps.
- Instagram Reels: plays, reach, average watch time, saves, shares, follows, profile actions, Story replies after reposting.
- Instagram carousels: saves, shares, slide completion, profile actions, comments/questions.
- Apple: product page views, impressions, conversion rate, custom product page performance, product page optimization winner, App Analytics campaign/source data.
- LinkedIn: impressions, dwell/engagement, profile/page follows, useful comments, teacher/music-business relevance.
- Facebook: group-safe engagement, comments from actual musicians/teachers, page follows, link clicks.
- Threads: replies, reposts, profile taps, conversation quality.
- Website/landing: source-tagged visits, CTA clicks, beta/App Store handoff, bounce signals, audience-page fit.

### 9. Learn And Recycle

Weekly retro questions:
- Which hook earned attention from the right audience?
- Which product action was easiest to understand without explanation?
- Which comments revealed confusion?
- Which pillar deserves more volume?
- Which asset should become a paid creative test?

## Launch Implementation Plan

### Sprint 0: Infrastructure

Goal: make the social system usable before heavy posting starts.

Tasks:
- Complete `docs/marketing/social-media/platform-account-setup-checklist.md` for TikTok and Instagram before native draft previews.
- Reserve/confirm handles across Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, Facebook, and Threads if desired.
- Align bios to the same core promise.
- Build link hub or website landing path that can later route to App Store custom product pages.
- Add a social media analytics sheet or scheduler dashboard.
- Create account profile images and cover assets from the canon logo.
- Build three reusable edit templates: TikTok/Reel demo short, before/after, Instagram carousel.
- Prepare a rights-safe demo chart library.
- Prepare Apple App Store badge and product-image usage notes for launch assets.

Exit criteria:
- TikTok and Instagram accounts are live, secured, and ready for native draft preview.
- Calendar template is filled with the first two weeks.
- Every planned post has a landing path and CTA.

### Sprint 1: Proof Library

Goal: create raw material for 30 days of posts.

Tasks:
- Capture 10 iPad screen recordings.
- Capture 5 physical Pencil clips.
- Export 5 clean PDFs from demo charts.
- Record 5 voiceover explanations.
- Pull 10 high-quality app screenshots.
- Produce 12 finished vertical masters.
- Draft 2 App Store app-preview storyboards from the strongest vertical workflows.
- Draft 3 custom product page concepts: bandleaders, teachers, and rhythm section/session players.
- Draft 3 longer-form social posts for LinkedIn/Facebook/Threads from the best early proof assets.

Exit criteria:
- At least 8 posts ready to schedule.
- At least 4 more posts have raw footage and scripts.

### Sprint 2: Beta/TestFlight Campaign

Goal: attract the right testers and learn messaging.

Content mix:
- 45% TikTok/Reels workflow demos.
- 20% before/after proof.
- 15% Instagram Stories, Threads, and build/test learning.
- 10% teacher or bandleader use cases.
- 10% direct beta CTA.

Success signals:
- Musicians ask to test.
- Comments reference real chart workflows.
- TestFlight feedback includes usable reproduction details.
- Saves/shares cluster around demos and before/after posts.

### Sprint 3: App Store Launch Campaign

Goal: convert awareness into installs and early trust.

Tasks:
- Use Apple-approved badge assets.
- Create audience-specific landing paths or custom product pages:
  - Bandleader: fast rehearsal prep.
  - Teacher: simplified student charts and clean PDFs.
  - Rhythm section/session player: roadmap, chart versions, and transposition.
- Run product page optimization once enough traffic exists.
- Convert the best organic posts into paid creative tests only after they show audience fit.
- Keep TikTok and Instagram campaign links separate so App Analytics can distinguish source quality.

Success signals:
- App Store product page taps and installs.
- High-intent comments.
- Trial/Pro starts if monetization is live.
- Clear learning from custom product page or PPO results.

## Baseline Cadence

Start with a cadence that can be sustained without lowering quality.

Weekly:
- 2 short-form videos adapted separately for TikTok and Instagram Reels.
- 1 Instagram carousel or image post.
- 1 LinkedIn, Facebook, or Threads post adapted from the strongest weekly proof.
- 2-3 Instagram Story updates.
- 1 Apple marketing asset task: screenshot copy, app preview storyboard, custom product page angle, or product page test note.
- 1 reactive/community slot.

Do not scale past this until the content calendar, asset library, and measurement review are running cleanly.
